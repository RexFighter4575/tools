How Mono Mind works

The idea in one line: your memory mostly fails at the moment of encoding — so this app trains attention first, then retrieval, and keeps you fed with things worth knowing.

Today

Your dashboard: start the daily session, review notes that are due, and see what's new from your sources since last time. The freshness check runs automatically when the app opens (needs internet, and works when the file is opened in a normal browser — Claude's preview sandbox blocks outside connections).

Practice · the four stages

1 · Attend — read a short passage under a gentle timer, fully single-tasked. 2 · Check — two questions on it, self-graded honestly. 3 · Encode — memorize 8 words using a named technique (linking, story, absurd imagery). 4 · Recall & reflect — type the words back, then write one line about what you learned. That line becomes a note and enters the review cycle. Passages are drawn from your topics, so the reps build general knowledge too.

Lessons

Eight short reads on the science: encoding vs retrieval, divided attention, depth of processing, the 10-second rule, retrieval practice, spacing, chunking, and sleep. One question each to make it stick.

Topics & Later

Ten subjects — design, cars, music, fashion, science (home of Ologies), film, tech, food, history, news — each with a few long-running sources. PIN what you follow, FLAG what needs action, SAVE items to a reading list. Everything lands in Later.

Notes & spaced review

Anything worth keeping goes in as a note in your own words. It resurfaces after 1, 3, 7, then 21 days — tap "Got it" to advance, "Again" to reset. This is spaced repetition applied to your own thoughts.
