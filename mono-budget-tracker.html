How Mono works

The full overview — what every screen does and how the math behind it works.

The big picture

Mono is built savings-first. Every paycheck, it sets aside your savings target and your bills on paper before you spend a dollar, so the number you see as spendable is already safe. Your job is just three habits: log what you spend, mark bills paid, and move money to savings when the app says you can.

Home · Safe to Spend

The vessel shows total savings filling toward your emergency fund. Below it, Safe to Spend is the one number to check before buying anything:

paycheck − savings target − bills (paid + still due this cycle) − everyday spending so far

If it's positive, you're on plan. If it goes negative, nothing explodes — the next paycheck resets the cycle — but your savings target is what slipped.

Spend

The + button logs anything in seconds. Tap an entry to edit or delete it. Income entries are informational; "To savings" entries move your balance up; everything else counts against your budget.

Budget & bills

Everyday categories reset every paycheck. A striped bar means over budget — information, not judgment. Bills are monthly: "Mark paid" logs the expense and checks it off for the month (tap again to undo). The 50/30/20 check compares your whole setup to the classic guideline.

Goals

Goals are earmarks inside your savings pool — the emergency fund is the default one. "Add to this goal" logs a savings transfer and tags it. The calculators update live: per-paycheck cost of any target date, and compound growth over years.

Learn

23 modules across four tracks (Foundations, Short, Medium, Long term) distilled from the 30 podcasts, books, and sites in Sources. Mark modules done to track progress — it syncs like everything else.

Your data & GitHub sync

Everything saves on-device automatically. For sync across your PC and iPhone, connect a private GitHub repo in Settings — the app then pushes a data file on every change and pulls the newest version each time it opens. Your token is stored only on your device and is never included in the synced file. Note: sync needs internet and runs when the file is opened in a normal browser (Claude's preview sandbox blocks outside connections).

Beginner tips

Log expenses same-day or they never happen. Don't tune categories for two full cycles — watch first. When Safe to Spend is healthy near payday, sweep the extra into savings. And read one module a week; that's the whole curriculum in six months.
